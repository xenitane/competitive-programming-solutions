## Solution
From the given string count the number of n's, e's, i's and t's, and finally just print what is the maximum you can build using these.
```pseudo
	\begin{algorithm}
	\caption{Nineteen}
	\begin{algorithmic}
		\Procedure{Nineteen}{$str,len$}
			\State $n \gets 0$
			\State $e \gets 0$
			\State $i \gets 0$
			\State $t \gets 0$
			\For{$p \gets 0$ \To $len-1$}
				\IF{$str_{p}==\text{'n'}$}
					\State $n \gets n+1$
				\ELIF{$str_{p}==\text{'i'}$}
					\State $i \gets i+1$
				\ELIF{$str_{p}==\text{'e'}$}
					\State $e \gets e+1$
				\ELIF{$str_{p}==\text{'t'}$}
					\State $t \gets t+1$
				\EndIf
			\EndFor
			\Return $\max(\max(\lfloor{n/3}\rfloor,i),\max(t,\lfloor{e/3}\rfloor))$
		\EndProcedure
	\end{algorithmic}
	\end{algorithm}
```
### Analysis
We need constant space and linear time to go over the whole string.
